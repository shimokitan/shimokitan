I've reviewed all your legal documents for Shimokitan. Here's my assessment:

## What's Solid

**Privacy Policy**: Excellent transparency about minimal data collection, clear third-party disclosures, and honest about the single-operator nature. The no-accounts, no-tracking approach is refreshing.

**Terms of Service**: Covers the essentials well. The Indonesian jurisdiction clause is appropriate given your location.

**Copyright Policy**: Smart "no-host" positioning and the thumbnail indexing analogy (like search engines) is legally defensible.

## What Needs Attention Before Launch

**1. Missing Critical Elements**

You don't have:
- **Accessibility Statement** (increasingly expected, especially if you grow)
- **Data Breach Protocol** (even minimal data needs an incident response plan)
- **GDPR/International Compliance Detail** - You mention "international data transfers" but Indonesia + US services + global audience = you should explicitly address EU visitors' rights more thoroughly

**2. Ambiguities to Tighten**

- **Contact Form Data**: You say Resend doesn't store it, but *you* receive the emails. Where do those go? Are they in Gmail/Outlook? That's storage. Be more precise.
- **Cloudflare Logs**: "Not under our direct control" is vague. State the retention period (usually 30 days) or link to Cloudflare's specifics.
- **Age 13 Cutoff**: Your content is clearly aimed at older teens/adults. Consider raising this to 16 or 18 and explicitly stating your target demographic.

**3. Future-Proofing Gaps**

- **Affiliate Disclosure**: Good, but you should add *when* these will go live (even "Q2 2026" or "TBD")
- **Community Guidelines**: Published in advance is smart, but add a note about notification process when features launch
- **AI/Algorithmic Decision-Making**: If you plan any automated curation, disclose it now

**4. Practical Concerns**

- **Single Email for Everything**: You're using legal@, registry@, and listing the same contact for privacy, copyright, and general inquiries. This could create organizational chaos as you scale. Consider a ticket system or at minimum, separate addresses with clear SLAs.

## Verdict

**For a solo-operator launch? This is 80% there.** You can launch with this, but I'd recommend:

**Must-fix before launch:**
1. Clarify contact form email storage reality
2. Add explicit statement about EU users' rights (even if it's "we comply with GDPR principles" with a link to lodge complaints)

**Fix within 30 days post-launch:**
3. Write 1-paragraph data breach protocol
4. Specify Cloudflare log retention
5. Add accessibility statement (even a basic one)

**Before community features:**
6. Expand Community Guidelines with specific reporting/appeal process
7. Add content moderation policy
