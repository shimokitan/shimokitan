## Shimokitan — Internal North Star

**Version 0.1 — Working Draft**

---

### What This Is

Shimokitan is a curated archive for Japanese creative culture — anime, music, games, and the people who make it. It exists to transform creative effort into permanent cultural legacy, and to do that without compromise.

The archive is human-only. No AI-generated content, no stolen assets. If a creator lacks the budget, Shimokitan helps them find a collaborator. That is a founding constraint, not a policy detail.

---

### Core Philosophy

**Grow Together.** The platform exists to serve creators, not extract from them. No gatekeeping. No one-sided profit. Follower counts carry no weight here — a 1K artist with meaningful Zines stands equal to a 2M one.

The platform itself acts as the deep shadow so the work shines bright: a terminal-dark environment built around consistent, professional presentation of vibrant media. The interface never competes with the content.

---

### How Creators Are Represented

Artists own everything. Full control over their pages, credits, and licensing. The ability to revoke at any time.

Ranking on the homepage is not driven by popularity. It is driven by Zines — personal reflections written by the community, not professional reviews. The weight assigned to each Zine follows an internal formula. This keeps the archive culturally grounded, not algorithmically inflated.

---

### The Two Collaboration Models

#### A2A — Artist to Artist

Any artist on the platform may opt into an "Open Collaboration" badge. This signals openness to working with other artists directly.

Shimokitan does not intervene here. The terms of collaboration belong entirely to the artists involved — their language, their agreement. The platform takes no position on the legality or structure of any arrangement. The badge is simply a visible declaration of intent.

#### B2B — Business to Artist

This is where Shimokitan acts as a direct intermediary.

Businesses — studios, labels, production houses — can access the platform's talent layer. When they do, they encounter artists carrying an "Open Hiring" or "Open Business" badge. These badges are not visible to the general public. They become visible only when a business has engaged with Shimokitan as a paying client.

When a business and artist move toward a contract, both parties sign their own agreement — Shimokitan does not author or intervene in that contract. However, both parties are also required to sign Shimokitan's own terms of service, which exist to protect against external threats and to define what constitutes harm to either party.

Payment is held by Shimokitan until the contract is fulfilled. If a party is wronged as defined by the signed terms, the client-to-artist contract fees are returned to the wronged party. This is not arbitration — it is a mechanical consequence of the terms both parties agreed to before signing.

---

### How It Sustains Itself

The archive is free for everyone, always.

Revenue comes from B2B engagement. Businesses pay Shimokitan to access the talent layer — a fixed platform fee plus the held contract value. The B2B model is deliberately invisible to the general community. Shimokitan functions like LinkedIn's hiring infrastructure but built for creative culture.

A dedicated inbox (`handle@res.shimokitan.live`) keeps professional outreach clean and separate from the archive experience.

---

### How It Grows

Slowly. Genuinely.

Growth happens by being present as a fan first — commenting, praising effort, joining communities, showing up before making any ask. When the moment is right, the ask is simple: *"I think this work is precious. Can I archive it?"*

The platform does not scale through tactics. It scales through trust.

---

### The One-Liner

*A curated, human-only archive that transforms creative effort into permanent cultural legacy — and provides the professional infrastructure to grow without compromise.*
