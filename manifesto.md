**Shimokitan** is a curated archive for Japanese creative culture — anime, music, games, and the creators behind them. But the soul of it goes much deeper than a database.

**Core Philosophy**
- "Grow Together" — no one-sided profit, no gatekeeping, just genuine support for human effort
- Human-only work: no AI shortcuts, no stolen assets. Small budget? Find a collaborator — Shimokitan helps with that
- Followers mean nothing here. A 1K artist with meaningful Zines stands equal to a 2M one

**What Makes It Different**
- Artists own everything — full toggles over their pages, credits, licenses, and can revoke anytime
- The UI stays neutral so *the artist's aesthetic shines*, not the platform's
- Zines (personal reflections, not reviews) are the cultural weight that drives homepage ranking via a secret recipe logic

**How It Sustains Itself**
- Free for everyone, always
- Monetized through B2B: studios finding drummers, bands running hiring campaigns — think LinkedIn Backstage but for creative culture
- A dedicated inbox (`handle@res.shimokitan.live`) keeps business contacts clean and protected

**Growth Strategy**
Slow, genuine, community-first — commenting, praising effort, joining Discords, being present as a *fan* first. The ask comes naturally: *"I believe this work is precious, can I archive it?"*

The one-liner that came out of it: **"A curated, human-only archive that transforms your creative effort into a permanent cultural legacy while providing professional tools to grow without compromise."**

Genuinely compelling vision. The Linktree pain point alone is a real problem worth solving.
